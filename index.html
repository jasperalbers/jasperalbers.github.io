About me

Hello fellow traveler of the internet! My name is Jasper, and I am a PhD student with Prof. Dr. Markus Diesmann and Prof. Dr. Sacha J. van Albada at INM-6, Research Center Jülich. My goal is to understand the function of brain areas in the context of neural computing by constructing and simulating biologically realistic neural networks. Focusing on the visual pathway, I model a spatially resolved multi-area network based on anatomical and physiological data, simulate and validate its dynamics, and infer functions by comparison to simplified models. Additionally, I strive to investigate the influence of learning on brain function, crucial for which are long simulations of large cortical networks on high-performance computers or purpose-built neuromorphic hardware.
